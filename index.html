About VocoSense

VocoSense is a non-invasive, portable, and cost-effective device that converts vocal-cord vibrations into clear digital speech, enabling individuals to communicate independently in daily life. Target customers include people with congenital neurological conditions (e.g., Childhood Apraxia), stroke survivors with aphasia, and those who have lost vocal function from surgery or trauma. Unlike bulky or surgically implanted alternatives, VocoSense combines a flexible, self-powered biosensor with a trained machine-learning model and a paired smartphone app, making it accessible across healthcare budgets. By translating throat signals into text or synthesized speech, VocoSense restores a reliable channel for expression ‚Äî a bridge to conversation, community, and dignity.

How It Works

Four simple steps to restore communication

1

Skin-safe biosensor

A patch-like sensor placed near the vocal cords captures acoustic throat vibrations using a hybrid friction-piezoelectric stack (PVDF + PDMS with copper/aluminum electrodes).

2

Signal acquisition

Sensor outputs are digitized and preprocessed by an acquisition module (MVP uses a wired acquisition card; planned wireless/Bluetooth in future iterations).

3

Machine learning

A CNN model trained on throat vibration samples extracts spectral features and classifies intended speech, converting signals into text with high accuracy.

4

Paired app

The mobile app shows recognized text for review/edit and plays synthesized speech. Additional features include translation and voice customization.

Validation & Progress

VocoSense has progressed from design schematics to a working prototype and controlled lab tests. We tested 10+ sensor architectures (piezoelectric, triboelectric, hybrid). The selected hybrid sensor (PVDF + PDMS with aluminum & copper electrodes) produced the strongest, most repeatable waveforms during standardized bending tests and could distinguish different kinematic inputs. Laboratory tests with a limited Mandarin vocabulary (numbers 1‚Äì10, vowels, six single-character words) yielded a CNN classification accuracy up to 99% under controlled conditions. Next steps: expand dataset across ages, genders, and real-world conditions; conduct wearability studies; integrate wireless transmission and on-device preprocessing.

Market Opportunity

The Problem

Communication is a fundamental human need yet millions lack affordable, natural ways to speak. Many stroke survivors develop aphasia and others are born with conditions that limit speech. Current assistive methods (sign language, typing AAC tools, implanted BCIs) either require others' knowledge, are slow, or are invasive and costly. VocoSense focuses on making silent-speech natural and accessible: a vibration-sensing patch translates vocal-cord signals into text/speech via a phone app, improving speed, privacy, and independence.

Market Size

TAM: up to ~1 billion people worldwide with speech and language difficulties. SAM (initial): clinical patients and rehabilitation centers in targeted regions. Initial GTM prioritizes hospitals, rehab clinics, and speech therapy centers (B2B) plus direct online sales and community outreach (B2C). Key buyer concerns: accuracy, affordability, comfort, and clinical validation.

Competitive Advantage

Competitors include traditional speech-generating devices, symbol/keyboard AAC apps, other silent-speech prototypes using neck sensors, and invasive BCIs. VocoSense differentiates by its flexible, self-powered wearable design, emphasis on daily comfort/usability, and a focus on making the solution affordable and widely deployable. Limitations: per-user training required, sensitivity to placement/movement; planned mitigations include improved UX guides, sensor mounting improvements, and robust ML personalization.
